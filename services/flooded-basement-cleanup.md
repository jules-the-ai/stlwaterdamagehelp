When water damage happens, quick decisions matter. Help for basement flooding, sump pump failures, seepage, flash flooding, and water coming through drains or foundation walls. The right restoration partner can help limit secondary damage, document the loss, and set up a drying plan before repairs begin.

## What this service may include

- Safety check before entering the basement
- Water source identification when possible
- Removal guidance for wet boxes, carpet, and porous materials
- Humidity control and dry-out monitoring
- Prevention tips for future storms

## When to call

Call or text when you see standing water, wet drywall, soaked carpet pad, sagging ceilings, musty odor after a leak, sewage, or water that may have reached electrical systems. If there is immediate danger, call local emergency services first.

## Local St. Louis considerations

Many St. Louis properties have basements, older plumbing, clay sewer laterals, heavy spring storms, and winter freeze risks. A local restoration response should account for building age, basement construction, humidity, and insurance documentation needs.
