A sewage backup is not the same as a clean-water spill. It can contain bacteria, viruses, and other contaminants, so the safest approach is to limit exposure and get qualified cleanup guidance.

## Avoid direct contact

Keep children, pets, and unprotected adults out of the affected area. Do not use fans that could spread contaminated droplets or odors through the home.

## Know what may need removal

Porous materials such as carpet pad, some drywall, insulation, and upholstered contents may not be safely salvageable after a sewage event.

## Disinfection follows removal and cleaning

Disinfectants work best after gross contamination is removed and surfaces are cleaned. A restoration company can determine the order of operations.

## Document the event

Take photos from a safe distance and record when the backup started, which drains were affected, and any plumber findings.
