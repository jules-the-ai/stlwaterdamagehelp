Mold prevention starts with moisture control. Public health guidance consistently emphasizes fixing water problems and drying wet materials quickly.

## Find and fix the moisture source

A hidden leak, recurring seepage, or roof issue must be addressed or mold concerns can return after cleanup.

## Dry materials quickly

Wet drywall, carpet pad, wood trim, and insulation can hold moisture even when the room looks dry. Moisture meters and dehumidification help confirm progress.

## Do not paint over the problem

Paint and fragrance do not solve moisture or mold. If materials are contaminated or remain wet, the underlying problem needs correction.

## Use containment when needed

Professional remediation may involve containment, air filtration, controlled removal, and cleaning methods that reduce cross-contamination.
