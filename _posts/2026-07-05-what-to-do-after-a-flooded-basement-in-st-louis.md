A flooded basement feels urgent because it is urgent. Start with safety, then documentation, then drying.

## 1. Stay out if electricity may be involved

Do not step into standing water if outlets, extension cords, appliances, or the electrical panel may be affected. If you are unsure, wait for qualified help.

## 2. Stop the source if safe

Shut off a broken supply line, turn off the main water valve, or address a sump pump problem only if it is safe. Stormwater and sewage situations may require professional response.

## 3. Document before cleanup

Take wide photos, closeups, and short videos. Capture the water line, affected flooring, wet contents, and any visible source.

## 4. Remove what you can safely move

Move dry valuables away from the affected area. Avoid handling sewage-contaminated items without proper protection.

## 5. Start professional drying quickly

Extraction is only step one. Basements often hide moisture behind baseboards, drywall, insulation, and flooring. A restoration partner can measure moisture and set up drying equipment.
